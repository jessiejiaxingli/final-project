In Douglass Rushkoff's book Present Shock, he discusses a symptom of our modern world called "narrative collapse".

He writes that narrative is a story that helps us understand our place in the world.

Now, with digital media, as we are constantly being bombarded with news from all over the world, there's a breakdown of our structered narrative.

We are all given different narratives to believe, and if we were to imagine technology to be a pair of glasses through which to view the world, it has been tainted by capitalism and corporations.

Our conventional sense of continuity has been hijacked by corporations, manipulating us into disillusionment with false promises.

In his words, "it felt less like existential despair when we had a story."

They have screwed up our realities.
