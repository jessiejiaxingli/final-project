When our narratives collapse, we are left without catharsis, without an end.

The purpose of narrative is to search for meaning, but without it, we are left without meaning. We become nihilistic.

The internet, as a never-ending database of information, resists the traditional narrative. Data is loosely-connected, if not unrelated, and subject to constant change and alteration. It doesn't obey the same rules of time that narratives do. In fact, it is indifferent to time. Similarly, it is indifferent to truth.

We start struggling with a sense of time in this age of information, and we each become responsible for shaping our own narratives.

Despite the fact that our traditional narrative tools cannot be used to make sense of the overwhelming amounts of information, we still attempt because that is what we've always done.

Thus, to create a narrative, we must delete "entries", or else the narrative could be easily upended...

...And we lose the ability to account for development over time, because the database that is the internet is indifferent to both time and truth.

So as we cling to our tentative and fragile personal narratives that are essential to our own identity, we defend it overzealously.

We leave no room for different voices.

And Then?
